UEFA Champions League Trophy

About the Trophy

The UEFA Champions League trophy is one of the most prestigious awards in world football. It is awarded annually by the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA) to the winner of the Champions League competition. The trophy is recognized globally as a symbol of excellence in club football.

History of the Trophy

The current UEFA Champions League trophy was first awarded in 1967. It features a large silver cup and two side handles. The trophy weighs approximately 7.5 kilograms (16.5 pounds) and stands 73.5 cm tall. It was designed by the Italian artist and silversmith, Silvio Gazzaniga.

UEFA Champions League Trophy

The UEFA Champions League trophy, designed by Silvio Gazzaniga, is awarded to the winner of the prestigious tournament. The trophy features intricate design elements that represent the high level of excellence in European football.
